<!-- The Designing Principle is the story's deep structural DNA.
     It answers: "What unique approach makes THIS telling of THIS premise
     different from every other version?"
     
     Examples:
     - "A murder investigation told three times from contradictory witness
       perspectives, where each retelling changes the audience's sympathy."
     - "A love story structured as a geological timeline, where each 'era'
       represents a phase of the relationship's fossilization."
     - "A heist that unfolds in reverse chronological order, revealing the
       crew's motivations only after you've seen the consequences."
     
     BAD: "A story about a detective solving a murder" (that's a premise)
     GOOD: "A detective story where the investigation method mirrors the
            killer's psychological disorder" (that's a Designing Principle) -->

<!-- Framework guide:
     
     TRUBY'S ORGANIC ARCHITECTURE (Character-Driven Focus)
       Use when: narrative is deeply rooted in a character's internal flaws.
       Key elements: psychological flaw → moral weakness → opponent competing
       for same desire → apparent defeat → final crucible.
     
     DRAMATICA THEORY (Objective/Subjective Split)
       Use when: complex, multi-perspective narrative is needed.
       Key elements: separate Protagonist (objective driver) from Main Character
       (subjective lens). Interweave four throughlines: Overall Story, Main
       Character, Influence Character, Relationship Story.
     
     KISHŌTENKETSU (The Conflict-Less Engine)
       Use when: building profound emotional resonance without adversarial combat.
       Key elements: Ki (introduction) → Shō (development) → Ten (twist that
       recontextualizes) → Ketsu (philosophical harmonization). The twist is
       NOT an explosion — it's a perspective shift.
     
     FICHTEAN CURVE (High-Tension/Thriller)
       Use when: goal is sustained elevated tension (cortisol) until final pages.
       Key elements: In media res (skip exposition) → escalating crises
       (two-thirds of story) → backstory woven into crisis reactions → climax.
-->

**Crucible:** [The moment of absolute tension where transformation is forced — describe the specific situation that makes the protagonist's hamartia unsustainable]

<!-- The crucible is the final "battle" or moment of absolute tension.
     It must force the protagonist to recognize their hamartia. The
     realization itself — followed by the emotional release (catharsis)
     of watching them transform or fail — must convey the meaning,
     purging the audience of pity and fear WITHOUT moralizing.
     
     AI tendency: Have the narrator explicitly explain the theme (77% of
     the time). This must be prevented. The crucible SHOWS; it does not TELL. -->

**Catharsis Type:** [Transformation / Tragic Recognition / Ambiguous]

<!-- Transformation:       Protagonist overcomes hamartia at severe cost.
     Tragic Recognition:   Protagonist recognizes hamartia but fails to
                           overcome it — permanent downfall. The AI is
                           explicitly authorized to write this outcome.
     Ambiguous:            The audience is left to determine whether
                           transformation truly occurred. -->

**Cost of Catharsis:** [The severe, irreversible price paid for transformation or the permanent consequence of failure]

<!-- Earned Catharsis requires severe, irreversible cost. The protagonist's
     final transformation (or tragic realization) cannot be achieved through
     deus ex machina or a simple change of heart.
     
     The Burden of Creation: A tragic downfall is permitted, but it must
     never be sudden or easy. If the protagonist fails, they must fail
     only after exhausting every possible avenue of agency and transformation. -->

**Most Obvious Resolution:** [What would generic AI write? What is the path of least resistance — the most predictable, clichéd outcome?]

<!-- As an LLM, the agent operates by predicting the next most likely token.
     If the logprobs of a narrative sequence are near certain, the narrative
     is highly predictable — synonymous with unoriginal, clichéd, and boring.
     
     The Logprob Override: The agent must actively reject the first, most
     obvious resolution to any conflict or scene. If a narrative choice feels
     like the path of least resistance, it must be discarded. -->

**Actual Resolution:** [How this story resists that entropy — what makes the real ending surprising yet inevitable, earned yet unexpected?]

**Entropy Safeguards:**
- [ ] The first obvious resolution has been explicitly identified and rejected
- [ ] If tragic, the downfall is not sudden or easy — protagonist exhausted every avenue first
- [ ] If triumphant, the victory is not clean — it exacts an irreversible cost
- [ ] The ending does not moralize or have the narrator explain the theme
- [ ] Ambiguity is preserved where appropriate — not all threads are neatly tied
